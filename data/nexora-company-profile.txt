Nexora: From Garage Project to Series B

Nexora was founded in Austin, Texas in January 2021 by Maria Chen, a former
machine learning lead at a large cloud provider. Chen started the company
after growing frustrated with how hard it was for mid-sized businesses to
get usable analytics out of their own data without hiring a full data team.

She was joined a few months later by David Okafor, who came on as
co-founder and Chief Technology Officer in April 2021. Okafor had previously
spent six years building distributed systems, and he leads Nexora's
engineering org today.

The company's flagship product, the Nexora Insight Platform, shipped its
first public version in late 2022. It lets non-technical teams query their
business data in plain English and get back charts and summaries instead of
raw tables.

In March 2024, Nexora acquired Nexora Analytics, a smaller startup that had
built a well-regarded forecasting engine. The acquisition folded Nexora
Analytics' forecasting technology directly into the Insight Platform rather
than keeping it as a separate product.

Nexora is headquartered in Austin, Texas, with a smaller engineering
office in Toronto that opened in 2023.

The company raised a $28M Series B in June 2024, led by Sequoia Peak
Ventures. This was Sequoia Peak's second investment in Nexora; the firm
had also participated in Nexora's 2022 seed round.

Nexora also announced a partnership with CloudScale Systems in September
2024 to offer the Insight Platform as a native integration inside
CloudScale's data warehouse product.
